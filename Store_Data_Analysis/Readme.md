# Store Sales Data Analysis 

---

## Project Overview

This project analyzes store sales data to identify key revenue drivers, customer purchasing patterns, seasonal trends, and business improvement opportunities.

The analysis focuses on understanding:
- Category and item-level revenue contribution
- Customer demographic impact on sales
- Seasonal demand patterns
- Payment preferences
- Customer satisfaction trends

The ultimate goal is to provide data-driven recommendations for revenue growth and operational optimization.

---

## Business Problem

The analysis addresses the following key business questions:

- Which product categories and items generate the highest revenue?
- How do age group and gender influence purchasing behavior?
- What seasonal trends impact sales performance?
- Which products are purchased most frequently?
- What is the overall customer satisfaction level?
- Which payment methods are preferred?

---

## Dataset Overview

- Total Records: 5,000 transactions
- Total Revenue: 1.21M
- Customer Count: 5,000
- Overall Rating: 3.78 / 5

### Data Dimensions:
- Customer Information
- Product Details

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### Univariate Analysis

- Revenue is highly concentrated in specific categories.
- Electronics generates the highest revenue (~760K).
- Top revenue-driving items: Laptops, Mobile Phones, Headphones.
- Most frequently purchased items: Sandals, Formal Shoes, Sneakers.
- Customer base is evenly split:
  - Female: 2,504
  - Male: 2,496
- Majority customers fall in the 18–45 age group.
- Digital payments dominate:
  - Card: 4,009
  - Cash on Delivery: 991
- Ratings are stable but moderate (3.7 – 3.9 range).

---

### Bivariate Analysis

#### Revenue vs Category
- Electronics is the dominant revenue contributor.
- Footwear and Sports contribute moderately.
- Beauty, Accessories, and Groceries show lower revenue impact.

#### Revenue vs Item
- Premium electronics drive revenue.
- Lifestyle products drive volume.

#### Revenue vs Season
- Slight revenue peaks in Spring and Winter.
- Seasonal demand affects planning and inventory.

#### Category vs Rating
- Ratings are consistent across categories.
- No category shows exceptionally high satisfaction.

## Key Insights

- Revenue is concentrated in the Electronics category.
- High-value products drive profitability.
- Footwear shows strong purchase frequency.
- Customer demographics are balanced.
- Digital payments are strongly preferred.
- Customer satisfaction is moderate but stable.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Expand inventory and marketing in Electronics.
- Cross-sell footwear and fitness products.
- Boost lower-performing categories via promotions.
- Improve customer experience to raise ratings.
- Use seasonal insights for demand forecasting.
- Encourage digital payment adoption with incentives.
- Implement continuous KPI monitoring dashboards.
